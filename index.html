ARIZONA SUNSHINE

In the overcrowded genre of VR shooters, with quite a few of the zombie variety already out in the wild, Vertigo Games have a take on zombie slaughter that makes enough tweaks to the formula to ensure that Arizona Sunshine stands head and shoulders above the competition.

BOOK TIME

Although it offers an expanded scope compared to the competition, Arizona Sunshine is still very much a wave based shooter. The twist? Rather than warping between areas you walk between them. That shouldn’t suggest a casual stroll, because this is anything but relaxing. You get some down time, allowing brief opportunities to stop and smell the virtual roses, but you will also have to clear out zombies along the way. This is more like training, or a buildup before the next big encounter. The rhythms of the game – two player campaign co-op and all – are reminiscent of a slower paced Left 4 Dead. These walks also ease you into the game world as it offers up some scavenging opportunities for rifling through abandoned.

SKYFRONT VR

Recently, Levity Play announced that its multiplayer first-person shooter, Skyfront VR, will release on Steam Early Access on November 10. Building upon the legacy of classic PC arena shooters, Skyfront VR is set to push the genre forward into the realm of VR that is a first of its kind;

BOOK TIME

There is a considerable hurdle for many people when it comes to VR. Be it nausea, dizziness, motion sickness, you name it.

Looking to tackle the hurdle that motion sickness poses for many prospective VR gamers – particularly in the FPS space – Skyfront VR boasts unique locomotion mechanics that help to center players and provide them with a more comfortable gameplay experience. Which is a nice prospect for those who’ve wanted to play VR, but couldn’t because of nausea.
